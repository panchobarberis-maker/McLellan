Were You Wrongfully Terminated?

California is an at-will employment state — but that doesn't mean your employer can fire you for any reason. If your termination violated anti-discrimination laws, was in retaliation for protected activity, or breached an implied contract, you may have a legal claim. Wrongful Termination FAQs

Wrongful termination occurs when an employer fires an employee for an illegal reason — such as discrimination, retaliation for protected activity, or in violation of public policy — despite California's at-will employment doctrine.

Yes. At-will employment does not protect employers from liability when the termination violates anti-discrimination laws (FEHA), constitutes illegal retaliation, or breaches an implied employment contract. At-will is a default rule with significant exceptions — not a blanket shield.

For claims under FEHA, you generally have three years from the date of the adverse action to file a complaint with the California Civil Rights Department before filing suit. Other claims may have different deadlines. Contact an attorney as soon as possible to preserve your rights.

Potential damages include lost wages and benefits, emotional distress damages, and in cases of malicious conduct, punitive damages. Attorney's fees are also recoverable under FEHA where the employee prevails.
