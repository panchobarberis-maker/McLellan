Is Your Workplace Hostile or Abusive?

While not every form of workplace bullying is independently illegal in California, bullying that is based on a protected characteristic — such as race, gender, age, or disability — constitutes illegal harassment under FEHA. Similarly, bullying that is used to retaliate against an employee for protected activity is unlawful.

Workplace Bullying FAQs

Workplace bullying is not always independently illegal, but when the bullying is based on a protected characteristic (race, gender, age, disability, etc.) it becomes illegal harassment under FEHA. Bullying used as retaliation for protected activity is also actionable.

A hostile work environment exists when an employee is subjected to severe or pervasive harassment based on a protected characteristic that unreasonably interferes with their ability to perform their job. The conduct must be both objectively and subjectively offensive.

If the bullying constitutes illegal harassment based on a protected class, and your employer knew or should have known about it but failed to take corrective action, you may have a claim against your employer under FEHA.

Document every incident — dates, description, witnesses, and any written communications. Report through your employer's internal complaint process. If the bullying is based on a protected characteristic or follows protected activity, consult an employment attorney promptly.
