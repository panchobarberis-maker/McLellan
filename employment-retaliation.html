Did Your Employer Punish You for Speaking Up?

California broadly protects employees from retaliation for engaging in legally protected activities. If your employer fired, demoted, or otherwise punished you for reporting harassment, filing a wage complaint, taking protected leave, or blowing the whistle on illegal conduct, you may have a retaliation claim.

Retaliation FAQs

Retaliation occurs when an employer takes an adverse action — such as termination, demotion, or reduced hours — because the employee engaged in a legally protected activity, such as reporting harassment, filing a wage complaint, or taking protected leave.

Protected activities include reporting workplace safety violations, filing wage and hour complaints, reporting harassment or discrimination, requesting FMLA/CFRA leave, participating in workplace investigations, and whistleblowing under California Labor Code § 1102.5.

Key evidence includes timing (adverse action occurring soon after the protected activity), inconsistencies in the employer's stated reasons, differential treatment compared to other employees, and documentation of the protected activity itself.

No. California Labor Code and Cal/OSHA regulations prohibit retaliation against employees who report unsafe working conditions to their employer or to Cal/OSHA. Such retaliation is illegal and gives rise to a claim for damages.
