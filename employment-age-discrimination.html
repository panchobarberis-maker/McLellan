Age Discrimination FAQs

California's FEHA protects employees who are 40 years of age or older from discrimination based on age in all aspects of employment, including hiring, firing, promotions, pay, and job assignments.

Signs include being passed over for promotions in favor of younger employees, being laid off while younger colleagues are retained, receiving comments about your age or retirement, being assigned less desirable roles as you get older, or being pushed out through a hostile work environment.

Age discrimination claims are prevalent in the technology sector, where there is often an implicit preference for younger workers. If you are 40 or older and believe you were treated unfairly because of your age at a tech company in Silicon Valley, contact our employment attorneys for a free consultation.

Potential remedies include back pay, front pay, emotional distress damages, and in cases of malicious conduct, punitive damages. Attorney's fees are recoverable under FEHA if you prevail.
