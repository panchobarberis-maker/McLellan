California's Fair Employment and Housing Act (FEHA) provides some of the strongest employee protections in the country against workplace discrimination. If you have been treated unfairly because of your race, gender, age, disability, religion, sexual orientation, national origin, or another protected characteristic, you may have a legal claim.

Workplace Discrimination FAQs

Workplace discrimination occurs when an employer treats an employee unfavorably because of a protected characteristic under FEHA, including race, gender, age (40+), disability, religion, sexual orientation, national origin, pregnancy, or marital status.

You can show discrimination through direct evidence (e.g., a discriminatory statement) or circumstantial evidence, such as a pattern of treatment, comparisons to similarly situated employees, or suspicious timing of adverse employment actions.

Under FEHA, you generally have three years from the date of the discriminatory act to file a complaint with the California Civil Rights Department. Contact an attorney promptly to preserve your rights.
